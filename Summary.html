Real-time UDP playout over a hostile relay — submission summary

Recommended playout delay for grading
delay_ms = 85

Graders use unseen network profiles. 85 ms is the lowest delay that stayed VALID on the harder practice profile (5% loss, up to 80 ms jitter). Do not submit 40 ms unless you know the network is mild — it fails ~50% of frames on profile B.

Architecture

harness source ──47010──► sender ──47001──► relay ──47002──► receiver ──47020──► harness player ▲ │ └──────── 47004 ◄── relay ◄── 47003 ┘ (NACK feedback)

Strategy

  • Primary: immediate duplicate copies (~94% of frames) to survive loss without RTT
  • Secondary: limited NACK (max 2 per seq) for frames without a duplicate
  • Receiver: reorder buffer + poll() deadline timer (skip on miss)
  • Dropped: end-of-block XOR FEC — too slow for low playout delay

Why 85 ms?

Minimum playout delay is roughly the network's delay_max_ms plus a small margin. Profile B has delay_max_ms = 80; at 80 ms we still saw 1.2% misses (over the 1% cap). At 85 ms, multiple seeds passed with 0.3–0.9% misses. Profile A works at 40 ms, but graders use unseen profiles — submitting 40 ms risks instant failure on a harder network.
